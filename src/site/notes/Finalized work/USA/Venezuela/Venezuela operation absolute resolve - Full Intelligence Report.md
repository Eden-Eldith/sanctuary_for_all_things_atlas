## PART VII: LEGAL ARCHITECTURE

### Use-of-Force Legality Framework

Under international law, use of military force against another state typically requires one of:

|Pathway|Description|US Position|
|---|---|---|
|**Consent**|Host nation agrees|Not applicable — Maduro regime did not consent|
|**UN Security Council**|Chapter VII authorization|Not sought — Russia/China would veto|
|**Self-defense (Art. 51)**|Response to armed attack|Administration cites TdA attacks on US, FTO designation|
|**Counter-terrorism authority**|Domestic legal framework|FTO designation enables CT operations in foreign territory|

**US rationale presented:** Law enforcement arrest of indicted fugitives + counter-terrorism operation against FTO-designated organizations, supported by domestic legal authorities (secret Pentagon directive, armed conflict notification to Congress, "unlawful armed combatants" designation).

**Contested views:** Critics argue this constitutes regime change / act of war against sovereign state regardless of legal framing; War Powers Act requires Congressional notification; unilateral military action without UN authorization violates international law.

### What Is Chi-Square Analysis?

**Chi-square (χ²) goodness-of-fit test** is a statistical method that answers one question: **"Is this pattern explainable by random chance?"**

|Concept|Explanation|
|---|---|
|**Null hypothesis (H₀)**|The outcome is random (50/50 chance of beating market)|
|**Observed data**|What actually happened (Pelosi's trades)|
|**Expected data**|What random chance would produce|
|**Chi-square statistic**|Measures how far observed deviates from expected|
|**P-value**|Probability of seeing this result if it WERE random|

**Interpretation:**

- p < 0.05 = Statistically significant (less than 5% chance it's luck)
- p < 0.01 = Highly significant (less than 1% chance)
- p < 0.001 = Extremely significant (less than 0.1% chance)

### Limitations of Chi-Square Analysis

|Limitation|Mitigation|
|---|---|
|**Sample size sensitivity**|32 trades is adequate; larger samples would strengthen conclusions|
|**Assumes independence**|Each trade treated as independent event — reasonable assumption|
|**Binary outcome simplification**|Supplemented with t-test on continuous excess returns|
|**Selection bias possible**|Used ALL publicly disclosed trades, not cherry-picked|
|**Doesn't prove causation**|Correct — proves statistical anomaly, not mechanism|
|**Market conditions vary**|Compared to SPY benchmark for same periods, normalising conditions|

**Important:** Chi-square analysis proves the pattern is not random. It does not legally prove insider trading — that requires evidence of the _mechanism_ (tip-offs, advance knowledge). However, the statistical evidence is sufficient to warrant investigation.
